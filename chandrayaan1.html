Chandrayaan-1

  • Launch Date: 22 October 2008
  • Launch Vehicle: PSLV-C11
  • Objective: Lunar remote sensing and discovery of water molecules
  • Status: Successful

Chandrayaan-1 was India's first lunar probe and marked ISRO's debut in planetary exploration. It was instrumental in confirming the presence of water molecules on the Moonâ€™s surface. The orbiter carried 11 scientific instruments from India and international partners, and operated for 312 days.

Key Achievements

  • Confirmed the existence of water molecules on the Moon.
  • Mapped lunar surface minerals and elements.
  • International collaboration with NASA, ESA, and others.
