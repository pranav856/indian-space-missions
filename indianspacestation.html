India's Own Space Station (Planned)

  • Planned Launch: 2030s (target)
  • Launch Vehicle: Human-rated LVM3, future heavy lifters
  • Objective: Permanent Indian crewed presence in low Earth orbit
  • Status: Announced, under preliminary planning

Indiaâ€™s own space station is a visionary project announced by ISRO, aiming to establish a permanent, crewed platform in low Earth orbit. The station will enable long-duration astronaut missions, advanced research, and international collaboration, marking a new era for India's space program.

Mission Plan

  • Design and construction of modular space station compatible with Indian launch vehicles.
  • Phased deployment: initial module followed by expansion for science, habitation, and logistics.
  • Support long-duration crewed missions, research in microgravity, and technology demonstrations.
  • Enable docking for future Gaganyaan missions and international cooperation.

Pre-launch Activities

  • Concept studies, design, and feasibility analysis by ISRO teams.
  • Development and testing of life-support, power, and habitat modules.
  • International consultations and technology partnerships.
  • Ground-based simulations and astronaut training for long-duration missions.

Launch (Future)

  • Expected in the 2030s, after initial Gaganyaan crewed missions.
  • Modular launches using human-rated LVM3 and future heavy-lift rockets.
  • Assembly in orbit with robotics and astronaut support.

In Orbit (Planned)

  • Permanent Indian presence in low Earth orbit.
  • Research in microgravity: medicine, technology, materials.
  • International cooperation and dockings for joint missions.
  • Training, experiments, and Earth observation from space.
