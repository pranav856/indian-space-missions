What is ISRO?

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is India’s national space agency, renowned for its innovative, cost-effective missions and rapid advancements in satellite and launch vehicle technology. Established in 1969, ISRO has launched satellites for communication, earth observation, navigation, and planetary exploration, making India a key player in the global space community.

Recent Achievements

  • 2023: Chandrayaan-3 lands near lunar south pole, a world first.
  • 2023: Aditya-L1 solar mission launched, India's first Sun observatory.
  • 2022: ISRO launches record number of satellites with PSLV.
  • 2019: Chandrayaan-2 orbiter continues to send valuable lunar data.
