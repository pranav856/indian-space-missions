Chandrayaan-2

  • Launch Date: 22 July 2019
  • Launch Vehicle: GSLV Mk III-M1
  • Objective: Lunar surface study and rover deployment
  • Status: Orbiter operational; lander failed landing

Chandrayaan-2 was India’s second lunar mission. It consisted of an orbiter, lander (Vikram), and rover (Pragyan). Although the lander failed to achieve a soft landing, the orbiter continues to send valuable data about the Moon’s surface and exosphere.

Key Achievements

  • Orbiter continues to study lunar surface and exosphere.
  • Advanced imaging and mapping of lunar terrain.
